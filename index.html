The Changing Court

Over the years, the way in which ‘Religious Freedom’ has been interpreted by the Courts has evolved and shifted, moving through distinct ‘Constituitional Landscapes’, as discussed in class. Through this site, I’ve examined cases fromvtwo periods in particular – the Second Landscape from 1840 to 1940, and the Third Landscape, from 1940 until the present day. Through this site, a timeline can be constructed, creating a visual element through which the differing views of religion can be seen.

Why this project?

In order to showcase the evolving views of Religious Freedom in this country - and the ways in which the partisan divide has played into and been reflected by the Supreme Court.
